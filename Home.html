About Toyota

Toyota is a Japanese multinational automotive company that was founded in 1937 by Kiichiro Toyoda. It has grown to become one of the largest car manufacturers in the world, known for its reliability, innovation, and commitment to quality. Toyota has built a strong reputation over the years by focusing on producing vehicles that meet the needs of customers across different markets. Whether it's fuel-efficient cars, durable trucks, or advanced hybrid technology, Toyota has always aimed to stay ahead of the competition. The company’s philosophy, known as "Kaizen," meaning continuous improvement, has helped it maintain high standards in production and customer satisfaction. With a presence in more than 170 countries, Toyota continues to lead the way in automotive innovation, sustainability, and safety, making it one of the most trusted brands globally.

Global Ranking

Over the years, Toyota has consistently maintained a strong position among the top automobile companies worldwide. It is often ranked within the top three largest car manufacturers by sales volume. In several years, Toyota even achieved the number one spot, selling millions of vehicles across all continents. The company's success is attributed to its strong focus on quality, customer satisfaction, and constant innovation. Toyota's introduction of the Prius, the world’s first mass-produced hybrid car, was a major breakthrough that boosted its global reputation. In recent global rankings, Toyota has been praised not only for its sales numbers but also for its efforts in environmental sustainability, with a growing lineup of electric and hybrid vehicles. The company's global presence, diverse product range, and commitment to technology have helped it maintain a leading position in the competitive automotive market for decades.

Best Seller

The Toyota Corolla stands as Toyota's most successful and best-selling model, renowned for its reliability, efficiency, and affordability. Introduced in 1966, the Corolla quickly gained popularity and, by 1974, became the world's best-selling car. Over the decades, it has maintained this status, with over 50 million units sold globally by 2021. ​The Corolla's enduring appeal lies in its consistent quality and adaptability. It has evolved through multiple generations, incorporating advanced technologies and safety features while maintaining its core values. The model offers a range of options, including sedans, hatchbacks, and hybrids, catering to diverse customer preferences.​ In markets like Egypt, the Corolla is particularly favored for its fuel efficiency, low maintenance costs, and comfortable driving experience, making it a top choice for both families and professionals.

Sports Car

The Toyota GR Supra is a standout in Toyota’s lineup of sports cars, known for its sleek design, thrilling performance, and racing heritage. Originally launched in the late 1970s, the Supra gained a strong following thanks to its powerful engine and smooth handling. The modern GR Supra, developed with input from Toyota’s motorsport division, delivers an exciting driving experience with a turbocharged engine, rear-wheel drive, and sharp cornering ability. It blends modern technology with classic sports car spirit, making it a favorite among driving enthusiasts. Its eye-catching look and roaring engine sound make it a true symbol of Toyota’s commitment to performance and innovation.
